Design for
the real world

As technology progresses and pushes the boundaries of what is achievable makes one think how AR can be a more common technology that more people could use in their everyday life. There are solutions such as the HoloLens already on the market but with its drawbacks on how the user has to tilt their head and aim their sight on the wanted input on a keyboard. We wanted to investigate if there is a better solution on how to interact and put in passwords to gain access in the real world with help of AR.

Improved
Security

The use of AR opens up the possibilities of how security can be improved. Layering is one option where AR can be seen as the first layer, you only get to see and interact with the access mechanism if you are authorized. A second layer could consist of adding randomness to the input so that shoulder surfing could be eliminated as the pattern of the password will be different from time to time.
